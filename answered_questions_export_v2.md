## 1. Topic: Organization - Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development

**Q:** A technology company has grown rapidly and needs to report both by product line and by technical function (e.g., engineering, UX design). Employees are currently struggling because they report to both a product manager and a functional manager, leading to conflicting priorities.

**A:** Advise the CEO to eliminate one of the reporting lines to simplify the chain of command.
**A:** Ignore the conflict as it is a natural part of organizational growth and will resolve itself.
**A:** File a formal grievance against the product managers for overstepping their authority.
**A(Correct):** Organizational structure that combines departmentalization by division and function to gain the benefits of both; results in some employees reporting to two managers rather than one, with neither manager assuming a superior role.

**Rationale:** In a matrix structure, managing the dual-reporting relationship is key. The HR professional should facilitate interventions (OD) to clarify roles and responsibilities rather than simplifying the structure (losing the benefits) or ignoring the conflict.

**Q:** Employee engagement survey data indicates a significant drop in morale and productivity within the engineering division. Leadership is demanding an immediate hiring freeze to 'solve' the issue, but you suspect deeper systemic cultural issues.

**A:** Implement a company-wide performance improvement plan (PIP) immediately to weed out low performers.
**A:** Agree with the hiring freeze to ensure fiscal responsibility and wait for the situation to stabilize.
**A:** Directly challenge the CEO in a public forum to advocate for a cultural change initiative.
**A(Correct):** Process of enhancing the effectiveness and efficiency of an organization and the well-being of its members through planned interventions.

**Rationale:** Organizational development involves planned interventions to enhance effectiveness and well-being. The best action is to initiate a diagnostic process (e.g., focus groups or root cause analysis) to understand the underlying issues before applying a 'fix' like a hiring freeze. Distractors represent premature escalation, policy rigidity without analysis, and poor communication strategies.

## 5. Topic: Organization - Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development

**Q:** A division manager is frustrated by delays in hiring and suggests that their department should handle all recruitment tasks independently of the HR department. You must explain the role of your team in the context of organizational structure.

**A:** Explain that HR is a line unit responsible for the direct production of the company's revenue.
**A:** Agree to let the manager handle recruitment to avoid conflict and maintain current relationships.
**A:** Immediately escalate the manager’s request to the Chief Operating Officer for formal review.
**A(Correct):** Work groups that assist line units by providing specialized services, such as HR.

**Rationale:** Correct Answer: Explain that HR functions as a 'staff unit' that provides specialized services to support line units in achieving their goals. HR acts as a strategic partner, not a competitor. Other options are incorrect because HR is a support function (staff), not a production function (line), and escalation is premature before explaining the value-add of the HR service model.

## 6. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** Your company plans to partner with an external staffing agency to manage a large group of temporary IT contractors for a six-month project. You are concerned about the legal liabilities associated with these workers, as your internal managers will supervise their day-to-day tasks.

**A:** Independent Contracting
**A:** Outsourced Service Provision
**A:** Employee Leasing
**A(Correct):** Situation in which an organization shares responsibility and liability for its alternative workers with an alternative staffing supplier; also known as joint employment.

**Rationale:** The scenario describes co-employment (or joint employment). Independent contracting refers to non-employees, and the other distractors do not accurately define the shared legal liability inherent in this specific partnership model.

## 7. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** Due to a severe economic downturn, the CEO has asked HR to prepare a plan to reduce the workforce by 15% across all departments. This action is not based on individual performance, but strictly on the need to ensure the organization's financial survival.

**A:** Performance-based termination
**A:** Selective reorganization
**A:** Functional attrition
**A(Correct):** Termination of employment of individual employees or groups of employees for reasons other than performance, for example, economic necessity or restructuring; also known as reduction in force (RIF).

**Rationale:** The scenario describes a Reduction in Force (RIF), also known as downsizing, which is defined by the termination of groups of employees for reasons other than performance. The distractors incorrectly imply individual performance or reorganization rather than the specific act of workforce reduction.

## 9. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**A:** Immediately process the developer as a full-time employee to avoid delaying the urgent project.
**A:** Consult with the department head to determine if the developer will be working on-site to build team culture.
**A:** Inform the developer that they must transition to a standard employment contract to ensure compliance with company policy.
**A(Correct):** Self-employed individuals hired on a contract basis for specialized services.

**Rationale:** Correct Answer: Conduct an analysis to determine if the individual qualifies as an independent contractor based on control and tax regulations. This is the first step to mitigate legal risk. Treating them as an employee prematurely is a policy violation; ignoring the classification is a compliance failure.

## 10. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** Your company uses a large third-party staffing firm to source warehouse workers. A recent audit shows that while the staffing firm handles payroll, your company managers exert significant day-to-day control over these workers' schedules and task assignments.

**A:** Do nothing, as the staffing agency is responsible for all legal liabilities.
**A:** Terminate the contract with the staffing agency immediately to avoid potential risk.
**A:** Advise the managers to stop supervising the workers altogether to prevent co-employment.
**A(Correct):** Situation in which an organization shares responsibility and liability for its alternative workers with an alternative staffing supplier; also known as co-employment.

**Rationale:** The situation demonstrates 'Joint Employment.' The HR professional must perform a risk assessment and coordinate with the legal department to ensure compliance with labor laws regarding shared responsibility and liability, rather than ignoring the risk or reacting with an abrupt cancellation.

## 11. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** Your organization is struggling to determine future staffing needs for the next fiscal year due to highly volatile market conditions. The data science team suggests using complex statistical models, but senior leadership is unfamiliar with these methods.

**A:** Ignore the data science team and rely exclusively on the CEO's intuition.
**A:** Wait for the next quarter's results to see if the market volatility settles before making any predictions.
**A:** Hire an external AI-consultancy to automate all staffing decisions without human oversight.
**A(Correct):** Use of information from past and present to predict future conditions.

**Rationale:** Judgmental forecasts involve using past and present information to predict future conditions, which is essential when data is incomplete or volatile. A blend of historical data and expert judgment is a standard SHRM practice. Distractors represent inaction (waiting), over-reliance on non-data sources, or excessive reliance on AI without human context (AI Readiness).

## 12. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** You notice that critical institutional knowledge is leaving the organization whenever senior engineers retire, causing significant delays in project onboarding for new hires. You decide to implement a system that captures, stores, and facilitates the transfer of this expertise across the team.

**A:** Succession planning
**A:** Performance management
**A:** Human capital auditing
**A(Correct):** Process of creating, acquiring, sharing, and managing knowledge to augment individual and organizational performance.

**Rationale:** Knowledge management (KM) is the process of creating, acquiring, and managing knowledge to improve performance. Succession planning focuses on people movement, not the capture of organizational data/wisdom.

## 13. Topic: Organization - Organization – Workforce Management

**Q:** Due to a sudden and significant shift in market demand, the executive team has decided that the company must reduce operating costs by 15% immediately. The CEO has requested that HR begin terminating the employment of staff in the regional offices, citing economic necessity rather than individual performance.

**A:** Draft individual termination letters for the affected employees immediately to ensure the cost-saving target is met by the end of the week.
**A:** Inform all employees of the impending reduction in force (RIF) via a general town hall meeting to maintain complete organizational transparency.
**A:** Wait for the executive team to provide a finalized list of names to ensure HR does not influence the selection process prematurely.
**A(Correct):** Termination of employment of individual employees or groups of employees for reasons other than performance, for example, economic necessity or restructuring; also known as downsizing.

**Rationale:** The BEST action is to conduct a thorough workforce analysis to identify the business case, legal risks, and impact on diversity and inclusion before finalizing the RIF. Premature escalation (immediate letters) risks legal non-compliance; over-transparency can cause panic without a plan; and waiting for executives abdicates HR's role as a strategic partner.
