### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Assignees
**A:** Employees who work outside their home countries.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Global integration (GI)
**A:** Globalization strategy that emphasizes consistency of approach, standardization of processes, and a common corporate culture across global operations.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Globalization
**A:** Status of growing interconnectedness and interdependency among countries, people, markets, and organizations worldwide.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Identity alignment
**A:** Extent to which diversity is embraced in management of people, products/services, and branding.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Local responsiveness (LR)
**A:** Globalization strategy that emphasizes adapting to the needs of local markets and allows subsidiaries to develop unique products, structures, and systems.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Multinational enterprises (MNEs)
**A:** Organizations that own or control production or service facilities in one or more countries other than the home country.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Near-shoring
**A:** Practice of contracting a part of business processes or production to an external company in a country that is relatively close (for example, within the same region).

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Offshoring
**A:** Method by which an organization relocates its processes or production to an international location through subsidiaries or third-party affiliates.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Onshoring
**A:** Relocation of business processes or production to a lower-cost location inside the same country as the business.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Outsourcing
**A:** Process by which an organization contracts with third-party vendors to provide selected services/activities instead of hiring new employees.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Process alignment
**A:** Extent to which underlying operations such as IT, finance, or HR integrate across locations.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Redeployment
**A:** Process by which an organization moves an employee out of an international assignment; can involve moving back to the home country, moving to a different global location, or moving to a new location or position in the current host country.

---

### Workplace – Managing a Global Workforce
**Q:** Repatriation
**A:** Process by which employees returning from international assignments reintegrate into their home country’s culture, conditions, and employment.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Annualized loss expectancy (ALE)
**A:** Expected monetary loss for an asset due to a risk over a one-year period; calculated by multiplying single loss expectancy by annualized rate of occurrence.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Conflict of interest
**A:** Situation in which a person or organization may benefit from undue influence due to involvement in outside activities, relationships, or investments that conflict with or have an impact on the employment relationship or its outcomes.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Contingency plan
**A:** Protocol that an organization implements when an identified risk event occurs.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Duty of care
**A:** Principle that organizations should take all steps that are reasonably possible to ensure the health, safety, and well-being of employees and protect them from foreseeable injury.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Hazard
**A:** Potential for harm, often associated with a condition or activity that, if left uncontrolled, can result in injury or illness.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Key risk indicators (KRIs)
**A:** Metrics that provide an early signal of increasing risk exposures for an enterprise.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Moral hazard
**A:** Situation in which one party engages in risky behavior knowing that it is protected against the risk because another party will incur any resulting loss.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Principal-agent problem
**A:** Situation in which an agent (for example, an employee) makes decisions for a principal (for example, an employer) potentially on the basis of personal incentives that may not be aligned with the principal’s incentives.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Residual risk
**A:** Amount of uncertainty that remains after all risk management efforts have been exhausted.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk
**A:** Uncertainty that has an effect on an objective, where outcomes may include opportunities, losses, and threats.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk appetite
**A:** High-level characterization of the amount of uncertainty (acceptable risk) an organization is willing to pursue or accept to attain its risk management goals.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk control
**A:** Action taken to manage a risk.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk management
**A:** System for identifying, evaluating, and controlling actual and potential risks to an organization.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk position
**A:** Organization’s desired gain or acceptable loss in value.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk scorecard
**A:** Tool used to gather individual assessments of various characteristics of risk (for example, frequency of occurrence; degree of impact, loss, or gain for the organization; degree of efficacy of current controls).

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Risk tolerance
**A:** Characterization of the amount of uncertainty (acceptable risk) an organization is willing to pursue or accept to attain its risk management goals, defined in a range above and below a target.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Single loss expectancy (SLE)
**A:** Expected monetary loss every time a risk occurs; calculated by multiplying asset value by exposure factor.

---

### Workplace – Risk Management
**Q:** Whistleblowing
**A:** Reporting of an organization’s violations of policies and processes by employees.

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility
**Q:** Compliance

---

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility

---

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility
**Q:** Ethics
**A:** Set of behavioral guidelines that an organization expects all of its directors, managers, and employees to follow to ensure appropriate moral and ethical business standards.

---

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility
**Q:** Governance
**A:** System of rules and processes set up by an organization to ensure its compliance with local and international laws, accounting rules, ethical norms, internal codes of conduct, and other standards.

---

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility
**Q:** Sustainability
**A:** Practice of purchasing and using resources wisely by balancing economic, social, and environmental concerns, with the goal of securing the interests of present and future generations.

---

### Workplace – Corporate Social Responsibility
**Q:** Triple bottom line
**A:** Economic, social, and environmental impact metrics used to determine an organization’s success.
