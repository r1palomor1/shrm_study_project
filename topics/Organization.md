### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Center of excellence (COE)
**A:** An organizational structure that leverages staff expertise in certain areas to improve the entire organization’s strategic performance.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Cosourcing
**A:** Arrangement in which an enterprise and a vendor share different tasks within a larger complex, often strategic responsibility.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Dedicated HR
**A:** HR structural alternative that allows organizations with different strategies in multiple units to apply HR expertise to each unit’s specific strategic needs.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Functional HR
**A:** HR structural alternative in which headquarters HR specialists craft policies and HR generalists located within divisions or other locales implement the policies, adapt them as needed, and interact with employees.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** HR audit
**A:** Systematic and comprehensive evaluation of an organization’s HR policies, practices, procedures, and strategies.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Outsourcing
**A:** Process by which an organization contracts with third-party vendors to provide selected services/activities instead of hiring new employees.

---

### Organization – Structure of the HR Function
**Q:** Shared services HR model
**A:** HR structural alternative in which centers with specific areas of expertise develop HR policies in those areas; each unit can then select what it needs from a menu of these services.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Chain of command

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Departmentalization
**A:** Way an organization groups jobs to coordinate work.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Formalization
**A:** Refers to the extent to which rules, policies, and procedures govern the behavior of employees in an organization.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Functional structure
**A:** Organizational structure in which departments are defined by the services they contribute to the organization’s overall mission, such as marketing and sales, operations, and HR.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Geographic structure
**A:** Organizational structure in which geographic regions define the organizational chart.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Line units
**A:** Work groups that conduct the major business of an organization.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Matrix structure
**A:** Organizational structure that combines departmentalization by division and function to gain the benefits of both; results in some employees reporting to two managers rather than one, with neither manager assuming a superior role.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Organizational development
**A:** Process of enhancing the effectiveness and efficiency of an organization and the well-being of its members through planned interventions.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Product structure
**A:** Organizational structure in which functional departments are grouped under major product divisions.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Span of control
**A:** Refers to the number of individuals who report to a supervisor.

---

### Organization – Organizational Effectiveness & Development
**Q:** Staff units

### Organization – Workforce Management

---

### Organization – Workforce Management

---

### Organization – Workforce Management

### Organization – Workforce Management

---

### Organization – Workforce Management

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Succession planning
**A:** Process of implementing a talent management strategy for identifying and fostering the development of high-potential employees or other job candidates who, over time, may move into leadership positions of increased responsibility.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Talent management
**A:** Development and integration of HR processes that retain the knowledge, skills, and abilities of employees that will meet current and future organizational needs.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Turnover
**A:** Act of replacing employees leaving an organization; attrition or loss of employees.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Turnover rate
**A:** Annualized formula that tracks number of separations and total number of workforce employees per month.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Workforce analysis
**A:** Systematic approach to anticipate human capital needs and data HR professionals can use to ensure that appropriate knowledge, skills, or abilities will be available when needed to accomplish organizational goals and objectives.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Workforce management
**A:** All activities needed to ensure that workforce size and competencies meet the organization’s strategic needs.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Workforce planning
**A:** Activities needed to ensure that workforce size and competencies meet current and future organizational and individual needs.

---

### Organization – Workforce Management
**Q:** Workforce profile
**A:** Part of workforce analysis that identifies the current make-up of employees in terms of their demographics, skills, competencies, performance levels, expected retirement dates, pay grades, and other factors that help explain the workforce’s composition.

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Alternative dispute resolution (ADR)
**A:** Umbrella term for the various approaches and techniques, other than litigation, that can be used to resolve a dispute.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Arbitration
**A:** Method of dispute resolution by which disputing parties agree to be bound by the decision of one or more impartial persons to whom they submit their dispute for final determination.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Constructive discipline
**A:** Form of corrective discipline that implements increasingly severe penalties for employees.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Grievance procedure
**A:** Orderly way to resolve differences of opinion.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Industrial actions
**A:** Various forms of collective employee actions taken to protest work conditions or employer actions.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Labor union
**A:** Group of workers who coordinate their activities to achieve common goals in their relationship with an employer or group of employers; also called trade union.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Lockout
**A:** Action of an employer to shut down operations to prevent employees from working.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Mediation
**A:** Method of nonbinding dispute resolution by which a neutral third party tries to help disputing parties reach a mutually agreeable decision; also called conciliation.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Picketing
**A:** Positioning of employees at a place of work targeted for the action for the purpose of protest.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Policy
**A:** Broad statement that reflects an organization’s philosophy, objectives, or standards concerning a particular set of management or employee activities.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Project labor agreement (PLA)
**A:** Agreement that requires specific contractors to accept certain conditions in project contracts, such as paying a fair wage and contributing to health insurance, pension, and training funds.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Secondary action
**A:** Attempt by a union to influence an employer by putting pressure on another employer, for example, a supplier.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Sit-down strike
**A:** Refusal by workers to work; also refusal by workers to leave their workstations, making it impossible for the employer to use replacement workers.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Sympathy strike
**A:** Action taken in support of another union that is striking the employer.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Trade union
**A:** Group of workers who coordinate their activities to achieve common goals in their relationship with an employer or group of employers; also called labor union.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Unfair labor practice (ULP)
**A:** Violation of employee rights; act prohibited under labor relations statutes.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Whistleblowing
**A:** Reporting of an organization’s violations of policies and processes by employees.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Wildcat strike
**A:** Work stoppages at union contract operations that have not been sanctioned by the union.

---

### Organization – Employee & Labor Relations
**Q:** Work-to-rule
**A:** Situation in which workers slow processes by performing tasks exactly to specifications or according to job or task descriptions.

---

**A:** Style of computing in which scalable IT-enabled capabilities are delivered as a service using Internet technologies.

---

---

### Organization – Technology Management

**A:** Business management software, usually a suite of integrated applications, that a company can use to collect, store, manage, and interpret data from many business activities.

---

### Organization – Technology Management

**A:** Act of deliberately accessing computer data without permission.

---

### Organization – Technology Management

**A:** Information technology framework and tools for gathering, storing, maintaining, retrieving, revising, and reporting HR data.

---

### Organization – Technology Management
**Q:** Information system (IS)
**A:** Way to collect, organize, store, analyze, and share data.

---

### Organization – Technology Management
**Q:** Social engineering
**A:** In a computer context, tricking a user into sharing information that can then be used to access systems.

---

### Organization – Technology Management
**Q:** Social networks
**A:** Online clustering of individuals in groups with common or shared interests.

---

### Organization – Technology Management
**Q:** Software as a service (SaaS)
**A:** Software that is owned, delivered, and managed remotely and delivered over the Internet to contracted customers on a pay-for-use basis or as a subscription based on use metrics.
